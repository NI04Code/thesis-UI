\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experimental_setup}

To validate the effectiveness of the proposed TOPSIS-based \texttt{ResourceDefragmentation} strategy, we conducted a series of experiments using a discrete-event Kubernetes scheduler simulator. The simulator faithfully reproduces the Kubernetes scheduling pipeline (Filter, Score, Reserve, and Bind phases) based on the official \texttt{k8s.io/kubernetes} framework (v1.21), allowing for reproducible evaluation under controlled conditions without requiring a live cluster.

\subsection{Workload: Google Borg Cluster Trace}
The experimental workload was derived from the publicly available Google Borg cluster trace (v3). A processing pipeline extracted task submission events and scaled normalized resource requests using a reference machine of 96 vCPUs (96,000 milliCPU) and 384 GiB RAM.

To create realistic multi-dimensional contention, pods were classified into three strata via stratified sampling: \texttt{HIGH\_CPU} (25\%), \texttt{HIGH\_MEM} (35\%), and \texttt{BALANCED} (40\%). Pod resource requests were then uniformly scaled so that total demand equated to $120\%$ of the cluster capacity on both the CPU and memory axes. This guaranteed that approximately $20\%$ of the pods could not be scheduled initially, creating a measurable baseline of contention for the deschedulers to resolve. A total of 5,000 pods were submitted per experiment with pod shuffling enabled (\texttt{seed=42}) to introduce realistic stochastic variation.

\subsection{Descheduling Policies Under Evaluation}
Five experimental configurations (D1--D5) were evaluated. All descheduler-enabled runs (D2--D5) utilized an eviction budget of $10\%$, permitting up to approximately 410 pod evictions per run.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{D1 (None):} Baseline control with no descheduling. Measures raw scheduler performance.
    \item \textbf{D2 (LowNodeUtilization):} Evicts pods from underutilized nodes (CPU $< 80\%$ and Memory $< 80\%$) to consolidate load onto busier nodes.
    \item \textbf{D3 (HighNodeUtilization):} Evicts pods from overutilized nodes (CPU $> 80\%$ or Memory $> 80\%$) to spread load, prioritizing the eviction of large pods.
    \item \textbf{D4 (ResourceDefragmentation):} The proposed TOPSIS-based multi-criteria eviction strategy.
    \item \textbf{D5 (RDG):} The Resource Defragmentation Greedy algorithm proposed by Guo et al. \cite{guo2025joint}, which uses correlation-based selection to correct per-node resource imbalance.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cluster Analysis Results}

The simulator produces a Cluster Analysis snapshot at three key phases of the pipeline. The analysis reports the \textbf{Allocation Ratio} --- the fraction of total cluster capacity that is allocated to running pods --- for CPU (MilliCpuLeft), Memory, GPU, and MilliGPU. Since this experiment uses a CPU/memory-only workload (no GPUs), the GPU-related metrics are zero throughout and are omitted from the discussion.

\begin{quote}
\textbf{NOTE:} The ``Allocation Ratio'' percentages reported by the simulator (e.g., \texttt{MilliCpuLeft: 96.1\%}) represent the fraction of total capacity that \textbf{remains free} (i.e., left unallocated). Therefore, a higher percentage means \textit{less} utilisation. To compute actual utilisation: $\text{Utilisation} = 100\% - \text{MilliCpuLeft}\%$.
\end{quote}

\subsection{Descheduling Phase (Phase 2)}

The descheduling phase reveals stark differences in policy behaviour:

\begin{table}[htbp]

D3 & HighNodeUtil & \textbf{407} & 407 & 23.2s & All 20 nodes flagged overutilised \\

\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Key Observations:}
\textbf{D2 (LowNodeUtilization)} found \textbf{zero underutilised nodes} because at $\sim$96\% allocation, every node exceeds the 80\% threshold on both CPU and memory. This policy is fundamentally mismatched with oversubscribed clusters --- it is designed for consolidation in \textit{under-loaded} environments.

\textbf{D3 (HighNodeUtilization)} aggressively evicted \textbf{407 pods} --- the maximum budget. It found all 20 nodes overutilised and evicted the largest pods first. Post-eviction CPU allocation dropped from 96.0\% to 82.2\%, and memory from 96.2\% to 58.7\%, indicating significant capacity was freed.

\textbf{D4 (ResourceDefragmentation)} performed only \textbf{31 surgical evictions} targeting nodes with high Resource Imbalance Index. The TOPSIS multi-criteria selection ensured each eviction maximally corrected CPU/memory imbalance.

\textbf{D5 (RDG)} exhausted its full budget of \textbf{410 evictions} across all 20 nodes. Post-eviction allocation dropped to 68.3\% CPU / 68.0\% memory --- the most aggressive resource liberation of any policy.

\subsection{Eviction Efficiency Analysis}

A critical metric is the \textit{rescheduling yield} --- how many previously-failed pods are accommodated per eviction performed:

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Eviction Efficiency and Yield}
\begin{tabular}{|l|r|r|r|r|}
\hline
\textbf{Policy} & \textbf{Evictions} & \textbf{Rescheduled} & \textbf{Yield (resched/evict)} & \textbf{Desched Failures} \\
\hline
D2 (LowNodeUtil) & 0 & 69 & $\infty$ (no evictions) & 0 \\
D3 (HighNodeUtil) & 407 & 113 & 0.28 & 4 \\
D4 (ResDefrag) & 31 & 215 & \textbf{6.94} & 0 \\

\hline

\end{table}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{TIP:} D2 rescheduled 69 pods despite performing zero evictions. These reschedules occur during the post-deschedule retry phase, where previously-failed pods are re-attempted. The modest success rate ($\sim$7\%) reflects transient scheduling order effects --- some pods that failed initially can succeed when retried in a different order.
\end{quote}

\textbf{D3 (HighNodeUtilization)} has the worst yield: 407 evictions produced only 113 net reschedules (0.28 per eviction). Additionally, 4 evicted pods \textit{failed to reschedule}, meaning the descheduler caused net harm to those workloads. The aggressive, priority-unaware eviction disrupted existing placements without creating targeted free space.

\textbf{D5 (RDG)} performed marginally better with a yield of 0.43, but still consumed its entire budget of 410 evictions. The correlation-based candidate selection provides some improvement over HighNodeUtilization's size-based selection, but the greedy round-robin evaluation across all nodes leads to unnecessary evictions from already-balanced nodes.

\textbf{D4 (ResourceDefragmentation)} stands out with a yield of \textbf{6.94} --- each eviction enabled nearly 7 previously-failed pods to be scheduled. The TOPSIS multi-criteria selection specifically targets pods whose eviction maximally corrects resource imbalance, creating large contiguous free regions that accommodate multiple pending pods.
